About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Heidelberg University, affiliated with the Institute of Computer Science. I am supervised by Prof. Dr. Artur Andrzejak .

My research lies at the intersection of Software Engineering and Artificial Intelligence, with a focus on trustworthy AI, interpretable machine learning, and large language models for code. Previously, I worked on efficient distributed deep learning and DNN offloading strategies for local–edge–cloud collaborative environments.

Research

My research focuses on developing efficient, reliable, and interpretable intelligent systems that bridge artificial intelligence and software engineering.

Research Interests:

  • Trustworthy and Interpretable Large Language Models – Mechanistic interpretability, knowledge editing, and reliability of LLMs for code.
  • AI for Software Engineering – Code generation, code translation, automated debugging, and intelligent developer tools.
  • Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) – Transparent and interpretable machine learning models.
  • Distributed and Edge AI Systems – Efficient DNN offloading and collaborative intelligence across local–edge–cloud environments.
  • Green and Efficient Machine Learning – Energy-aware AI systems for IoT and resource-constrained environments.

My work has been published in leading venues such as ICLR, IEEE Transactions on Services Computing, IEEE Transactions on Green Communications and Networking, and IEEE Consumer Electronics Magazine.
